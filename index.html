Welcome

My name is Jonah Tatterson, and I am a senior computer science student at the University of Colorado Boulder. I created this portfolio as a way to present more than just the bullet points on my resume. I wanted one place that shows who I am, what I have worked on, and what I am interested in beyond classes and technical experience alone. Computer science is a major part of my life, but it is not the only thing that defines me. A lot of what I enjoy sits at the intersection of technology, creativity, discipline, and communication, and I wanted this site to reflect all of that in a way that feels personal but still professional.

Throughout college, I have built a strong foundation in programming, software development, and technical problem solving through coursework, projects, and internship experience. My academic background has helped me grow in areas like coding, debugging, systems thinking, and software design, while my internship work has shown me how those skills apply in real professional settings. At the same time, some of the most important parts of who I am come from outside the classroom. Rock climbing and running have taught me patience, consistency, and how to stay committed even when progress is slow. Video editing and content creation have helped me strengthen my creativity, communication, and ability to present ideas in a way that connects with other people.

This portfolio is meant for recruiters, employers, professors, classmates, and anyone else who wants to learn more about me and my work. I want it to show both my technical background and the broader interests that shape the way I approach challenges. Visitors to this site will find my resume, highlights of my academic and professional experience, extracurricular interests that matter to me, and links to platforms where I share more of my work and professional presence. My goal is for this portfolio to feel clear, approachable, and professional while giving a better sense of my strengths, interests, and the kind of work I enjoy doing.

Highlights of Interests / Extracurricular

Rock Climbing

Rock climbing is one of the most important activities in my life outside of school. It has taught me patience, consistency, and problem solving in a way that feels very real because every climb is different. Climbing forces me to stay focused, make adjustments in the moment, and keep working through challenges even when the solution is not obvious right away. It has also been one of the best ways for me to stay balanced when school becomes stressful, and it reflects a big part of who I am because I genuinely enjoy pushing myself to improve over time.

Running and Fitness

Running has become another major part of my routine and has shaped the way I think about long-term progress. What I like most about it is that improvement usually comes from small, repeated effort over time rather than instant results. Running helps me stay disciplined, clear my head, and build mental toughness, and those same lessons carry over into school, work, and other long-term goals. It is something I value because it reminds me that consistency matters, and that steady progress is usually what leads to real improvement.

Video Editing and Content Creation

Video editing and content creation show a more creative side of me that would not always be obvious from my resume alone. I enjoy making both short-form and long-form content around topics I find interesting because it combines planning, scripting, editing, and thinking about how an audience will respond. This has helped me strengthen my communication and storytelling skills while also pushing me to think more carefully about how ideas are presented. I value this part of my work because it reflects my interest in both technical problem solving and creative expression.

Highlights of Academic / Projects / Working

Computer Science Coursework and Projects

My coursework in computer science has helped me build a strong foundation in programming, software design, and technical problem solving. Through classes like Algorithms, Data Structures, Computer Systems, Discrete Mathematics, Principles of Programming Languages, and Principles of Software Development, I have gained experience thinking carefully about how systems should be built and how to approach more complex technical challenges. Along with coursework, I have also worked on independent projects that let me apply those skills in a more direct way. These experiences show the technical base I have been building throughout college and the progress I have made over time.

Accessibility and Web Design Work

One of the academic areas that has stood out to me most has been accessibility and web design work. This type of work changed the way I think about websites because it is not just about making something look good, but making sure people can actually use it effectively. Working with accessibility and usability made me think more carefully about design decisions, organization, and how users interact with digital spaces. It also helped me understand how thoughtful design can improve the experience for a much wider range of people. I think this is an important part of my portfolio because it reflects both technical skills and a stronger awareness of user experience.

Internship and Professional Experience

My internship experience has been one of the most valuable parts of my professional growth because it gave me the chance to apply what I learned in school to real working environments. As a Data Science Intern at Sea to Summit, I worked with Python, collaborated with teams, and supported data-related work used for analysis and decision making. As a Software Development Intern at Boulder IQ, I worked on prototype app development involving Raspberry Pi, Arduino, and wireless electrical signal display. These experiences helped me better understand teamwork, technical expectations in industry, and how classroom knowledge translates into practical work.
