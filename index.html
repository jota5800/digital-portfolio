Welcome

My name is Jonah Tatterson, and I am a senior computer science student at the University of Colorado Boulder. I created this portfolio to have one place that shows more than just the bullet points on my resume. I wanted a space where I could present both my technical background and the interests that make me a more well-rounded person. While computer science is a big part of what I do, it is not the only thing that defines me. A lot of my interests sit at the intersection of problem solving, creativity, communication, and discipline, and I wanted this portfolio to reflect that.

Throughout college, I have built a foundation in programming, software design, and technical problem solving through coursework, projects, and internship experience. My experience includes internships in data science and software development, where I worked with Python, database-related tasks, and prototype application development. At the same time, some of the things that matter most to me happen outside of the classroom. Rock climbing and running have taught me a lot about patience, consistency, and staying committed even when progress is slow. Creative work like video editing and content creation has helped me become better at storytelling, communication, and thinking about how to present ideas in a way that connects with other people.

The purpose of this portfolio is to create a more personal and professional introduction for recruiters, employers, professors, classmates, and collaborators. Visitors to this site will find my resume, highlights of my academic and professional experience, some of my extracurricular interests, and links to platforms where I share more of my work and professional presence. My goal is for this portfolio to feel clear, approachable, and professional while giving visitors a strong sense of the kind of work I enjoy and the value I could bring to future opportunities.

Highlights of Interests / Extracurricular

Rock Climbing

Rock climbing is one of the most important activities in my life outside of school. It has taught me a lot about patience, consistency, and problem solving. Every climb is different, which means I constantly have to adjust, think ahead, and stay focused in the moment. It is also something that helps me stay balanced when life gets stressful, because it gives me a challenge that is both physical and mental. Climbing reflects an important part of who I am because it shows that I enjoy working through difficult problems and that I am willing to keep improving over time instead of expecting instant results.

Running and Fitness

Running has become another major part of my routine and has shaped the way I think about progress. What I like most about running is that improvement usually comes from small, repeated effort over time. It helps me clear my head, stay disciplined, and build mental toughness. That mindset carries over into school, projects, and long-term goals because it reminds me that meaningful progress does not always happen quickly. Running is worth highlighting in my portfolio because it shows that I care about self-improvement, structure, and staying committed even when progress feels slow.

Video Editing and Content Creation

Video editing and content creation show a more creative side of me that would not always be obvious from just looking at a computer science resume. I enjoy making both short-form and long-form content around topics I find interesting because it combines planning, scripting, editing, and audience awareness. This kind of work has helped me improve at communication and storytelling, and it has made me think more about how to present ideas clearly and in a way that connects with people. I think this belongs in my portfolio because it shows that I am interested in both technical work and creative problem solving.

Highlights of Academic / Projects / Work

Computer Science Coursework and Projects

As a computer science student, I have spent the past several years building a stronger understanding of programming, software structure, and problem solving. My coursework has included algorithms, data structures, computer systems, discrete mathematics, principles of programming languages, and principles of software development. These classes helped me strengthen both my technical foundation and the way I think through complex problems. In addition to coursework, I have also worked on independent projects that let me apply those skills more directly. Those experiences matter because they show steady growth over time and reflect the technical base I have been building throughout college.

Accessibility and Web Design Work

One academic area that has stood out to me is accessibility and web design work. This type of work changed the way I think about digital experiences because it is not just about making something look good, but making sure people can actually use it effectively. Working with accessibility and usability has shown me how thoughtful design choices can improve an experience for a much wider range of users. It has also helped me think more carefully about communication, organization, and how people interact with technology. This is an important highlight because it shows that I value both function and user experience, not just code for its own sake.
